Intel Growth & Sustainability Roadmap

Intel's Journey: Growth, Innovation, and Sustainability

1968

Intel was founded by Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore, marking the birth of the microprocessor era.

Intel foundation.

1971

Intel introduces the 4004, the world’s first commercial microprocessor, shrinking computers into smaller, more energy-efficient devices. Early chips showed that scaling transistors improved performance and reduced material usage—laying groundwork for sustainable computing.

4004 microprocessor.

1985

Intel launches the 80386 processor, enabling advanced multitasking with better energy per operation. Manufacturing efficiency improvements begin, cutting waste and optimizing cleanroom practices.

80386 processor.

1992

Intel opens the first ISO 14001-certified environmental management system in the semiconductor industry, setting a new standard for environmental responsibility.

ISO 14001

1995

Pentium Pro processors are introduced, delivering higher performance-per-watt ratios and advancing energy efficiency in computing.

Pentium Pro processors

1998

Intel publishes its first Environmental, Health, and Safety report, establishing early transparency standards in the technology sector.

First EHS report

2001

Intel commits to reducing per-chip energy use, aligning Moore’s Law with energy efficiency and setting ambitious sustainability goals for operations.

Cut energy per chip use, aligning Moore’s Law

2008

Intel launches the Intel Core™ processors, achieving major gains in performance-per-watt and furthering energy-efficient computing.

Intel Core™ processors

2009

Intel becomes the largest voluntary purchaser of green power in the U.S., setting a precedent for corporate renewable energy adoption.

Green power approach

2010

Intel manufactures the first conflict-free microprocessors, ensuring ethical sourcing of tin, tungsten, tantalum, and gold.

First conflict-free microprocessors

2012

Intel begins large-scale water recycling projects, returning billions of gallons of water to local communities.

Water recycling projects

2014

Intel achieves 100% conflict-free minerals in all products, advancing ethical supply chain practices.

100% conflict-free minerals

2017

Intel reaches 80% renewable electricity usage globally, making significant progress toward its sustainability goals.

80% renewable electricity usage

2018

Intel achieves net positive water use in the U.S. and India, returning more water to communities than it consumes.

Net positive water use

2020

Intel pledges to achieve 100% renewable electricity globally by 2030, net positive water use, and zero waste to landfill.

100% renewable electricity

2021

Intel publishes its RISE strategy (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling), setting measurable ESG goals for the next decade.

RISE strategy

2022

Intel announces a $300M global R&D investment for sustainable semiconductor manufacturing, accelerating innovation in green technology.

Investment for sustainable semiconductor

2023

Intel reaches 93% renewable electricity worldwide and restores nearly 9 billion gallons of water to local communities.

Massive recycling

2024

Intel expands AI for Climate Research initiatives, enabling smarter grids, renewable energy forecasting, and sustainable farming.

AI for Climate Research initiatives
